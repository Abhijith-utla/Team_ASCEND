Your solution is almost correct, but it seems that you want to convert the list of integers to a list of characters. In Python, you can convert an integer to a character using the chr() function. However, in your case, you want to replace the space character with an empty string, and then convert the list of integers to a list of characters. Here is the correct solution:

```python
from typing import List

def sat(li: List[int]):
    s = ''.join(chr(i) for i in li)
    return s == "The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog"
```

The ''.join(chr(i) for i in li) part is using a generator expression to convert each integer to a character, and then joining them into a string. This should give you the correct answer.
